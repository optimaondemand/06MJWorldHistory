Ramesses III's Medinet Habu Inscription
Primary Source Reading
💡 ABOUT THIS SOURCE
Adapted into plain modern English from early Egyptological translations of the inscription Ramesses III had carved on the walls of his mortuary temple at Medinet Habu, describing Egypt's defense against a major Sea Peoples assault, likely around 1175 BC.
📖 THE INSCRIPTION

On the scale of the crisis beyond Egypt's own borders:
"The foreign countries made a conspiracy in their islands. All at once the lands were removed and scattered in the conflict. No land could stand before their arms, from Hatti, Kode, Carchemish, Arzawa, and Cyprus onward, all being cut off at one time. They set up a camp in one place inside Amurru. They desolated its people, and its land was as if it had never come into being at all. They were coming onward toward Egypt, while the flame was prepared before them."

On the composition of the invading coalition:
"Their confederation included the Peleset, the Tjeker, the Shekelesh, the Denyen, and the Weshesh, lands united together, and they laid their hands upon the lands as far as the circuit of the earth, their hearts confident and trusting, saying, 'Our plan will succeed.'"

On Egypt's preparation for the assault:
"I organized my frontier in Djahy, prepared before them: princes, garrison commanders, and charioteers, equipped with all the weapons of war. The ships, the boats, and the barges were manned completely from bow to stern with brave fighters bearing their weapons, and the infantry consisted of all the pick of Egypt."

On the battle itself, land and sea together:
"Those who reached my frontier, their seed exists no longer; their heart and their soul are finished forever. As for those who banded together upon the sea, the full flame was in front of them at the mouths of the rivers, while a wall of metal, bristling with lances, surrounded them on the shore. They were dragged in, enclosed, and prostrated on the beach, slain, and made into heaps from head to tail."

A closing claim of total victory:
"I have made the lands turn back from even mentioning Egypt, for when they say the name of my majesty in their land, it burns their bodies."
