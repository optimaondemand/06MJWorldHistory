Pylos's "Rower Tablets" (Linear B)
Primary Source Reading
💡 ABOUT THIS SOURCE
Adapted into plain modern English from scholarly translations of the Linear B tablets recovered from the palace of Pylos, hardened and preserved by the fire that destroyed the palace around 1180 BC. Unlike a royal inscription or an epic poem, these are terse, formulaic administrative records, lists of names, places, and numbers, never intended for anyone beyond the palace's own bureaucracy. Modern scholars refer to this group informally as the "rower tablets" and the related "o-ka" (watch-post) tablets.
📖 THE TABLETS

A coastal watch assignment (an "o-ka" tablet):
"Thus the watchers are guarding the coastal region: at Owitono, the company under the command of the officer Maleus: thirty men of the followers, together with a detachment of Kyparissian men assigned alongside them. At another post, the company under the officer Nedwatas: twenty men, together with men described as 'strangers,' likely refugees or foreign auxiliaries incorporated into the local defense."

A rowing-service muster (a "rower" tablet):
"Thus the rowers are to proceed to Pleuron: from Meta-pa, six men are absent and have not been supplied; from Pi-swa, absent, none supplied; from Re-u-ko-to-ro, absent, ten men to be supplied. The total of rowers still owed, gathered from these coastal districts, is recorded and to be followed up."

A shorter list, place and number only:
"Ro-o-wa: two men. Timito-akee: ten men. A-po-ne-we: thirty men. The men of Oikhalia are separately noted as still due."

💡 A NOTE ON WHAT THESE RECORDS DO NOT SAY
Nowhere in any surviving tablet does the Pylos administration state outright why these coastal watches and rowing musters were being organized, or name any specific enemy it feared. Historians infer the likely purpose, defense against a seaborne threat, from the timing (these are among the last records the palace ever produced) and from what we now know happened afterward, not from any explicit statement in the tablets themselves.
