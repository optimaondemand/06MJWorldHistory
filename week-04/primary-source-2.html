The Prologue to the Code of Hammurabi
Primary Source Reading
💡 ABOUT THIS SOURCE
Excerpted and lightly adapted from L.W. King's 1910 English translation of the Code of Hammurabi, which is in the public domain. This is the opening statement Hammurabi had inscribed at the top of his law stele, before the 282 laws themselves.
📖 THE TEXT

When Anu the Sublime, King of the Anunaki, and Bel, the lord of Heaven and Earth, who fixed the destinies of the land, committed the rule of all mankind to Marduk, the chief son of Ea, when they pronounced the lofty name of Babylon, when they made it famous among the quarters of the world and set up an everlasting kingdom within it, whose foundations were firm as heaven and earth, at that time Anu and Bel called by name me, Hammurabi, the exalted prince, who feared God, to bring about the rule of righteousness in the land, to destroy the wicked and the evil-doers, so that the strong should not harm the weak, so that I should rule over the black-headed people like Shamash, and enlighten the land, to further the well-being of mankind.

Hammurabi, the governor named by Bel, am I, who brought about plenty and abundance, who made everything complete for Nippur and Ekur, the guardian of Eridu, who purified the worship of Eridu, who conquered the four quarters of the world, who made the name of Babylon great, who rejoiced the heart of Marduk his lord, the powerful king who restored Uruk to its place, who supplied water in abundance to its inhabitants, the king who made the whole land to hold together, who brought the scattered peoples into safety, who provided pasture and water for their cattle, who feeds the temples with rich offerings, who cares for the welfare of the people, who established law and justice in the land.

When Marduk sent me to rule over men, to give the protection of right to the land, I did right and righteousness, and brought about the well-being of the oppressed.

Let any oppressed man who has a cause come into the presence of my statue as king of righteousness, and let him read the inscription, and understand my precious words, and may this inscription make the case clear to him, may he understand his cause, and may his heart be set at ease. Let the oppressed man, who has a case at law, come and stand before this my image as king of righteousness, and read the inscription, and understand my precious words, and find out what is his right.

I am Hammurabi, the shepherd, chosen of the god Bel, who has established law and justice in the land, so that the strong might not oppress the weak, and that they should give justice to the orphan and the widow.
