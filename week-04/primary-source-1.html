💡 ABOUT THIS SOURCE
Excerpted and lightly adapted from L.W. King's 1910 English translation of the Code of Hammurabi, which is in the public domain. The full Code contains 282 laws; the selection below illustrates its range across property, family, professional liability, and its class-differentiated penalties. Law numbers follow the standard numbering used by historians.
📖 THE LAWS

Law 6. If any one steal the property of a temple or of the court, he shall be put to death, and also the one who receives the stolen thing from him shall be put to death.

Law 21. If any one break a hole into a house, he shall be put to death before that hole and buried.

Law 108. If a tavern-keeper does not accept grain according to gross weight in payment of drink, but takes money, and the price of the drink is less than that of the grain, she shall be convicted and thrown into the water.

Law 129. If a man's wife be surprised with another man, both shall be tied and thrown into the water, but the husband may pardon his wife and the king his slaves.

Law 196. If a man put out the eye of another man, his eye shall be put out.

Law 197. If he break another man's bone, his bone shall be broken.

Law 198. If he put out the eye of a freed man (a mushkenum), or break the bone of a freed man, he shall pay one gold mina.

Law 199. If he put out the eye of a man's slave, or break the bone of a man's slave, he shall pay one-half of the slave's value.

Law 200. If a man knock out the teeth of his equal, his teeth shall be knocked out.

Law 201. If he knock out the teeth of a freed man, he shall pay one-third of a gold mina.

Law 215. If a physician makes a large incision with an operating knife and cures a man, or opens a growth with an operating knife and saves the eye, he shall receive ten shekels of silver in money.

Law 218. If a physician makes a large incision with an operating knife and kills the patient, or opens a growth with an operating knife and destroys the eye, his hands shall be cut off.

Law 229. If a builder builds a house for a man and does not make its construction firm, and the house which he has built collapses and causes the death of the owner of the house, that builder shall be put to death.

Law 233. If a builder builds a house and does not make its foundations secure, and it falls in and destroys property, he shall rebuild the house and bear the cost from his own property.

Law 245. If any one hires an ox and kills it by bad treatment or blows, he shall compensate the owner with an ox of equal value.

Law 250. If an ox, when passing through the streets, gores a man to death, no penalty is incurred if the ox was not known to be vicious.
