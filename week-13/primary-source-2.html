Nebuchadnezzar II's Building Inscriptions (on the Ishtar Gate)
Primary Source Reading
💡 ABOUT THIS SOURCE
Adapted into plain modern English from scholarly translations of Nebuchadnezzar II's royal building inscriptions describing his reconstruction of Babylon, including the Ishtar Gate and the Etemenanki ziggurat.
📖 THE INSCRIPTIONS

On the Ishtar Gate itself:
"I, Nebuchadnezzar, king of Babylon, built the Ishtar Gate with blue-glazed bricks, and set upon it the images of bulls and dragons, creatures sacred to the gods Adad and Marduk, that all who entered my city might know its greatness and the favor the gods have shown it."

On rebuilding the city's temples and the Etemenanki:
"I raised the top of the temple tower of Babylon toward heaven, that its head might reach the sky, and I caused the city's temples to shine like the sun, adorned with gold and precious stone, for the glory of Marduk, my lord."

On the purpose of these projects, in the king's own words:
"I have made Babylon a wonder for all peoples, a city whose splendor no other city on earth can equal, that my name might endure and that the gods might look with favor upon my reign."

📝 EDITORIAL NOTE
Nebuchadnezzar's building inscriptions survive in several versions, carved on foundation deposits and monuments across Babylon; the details presented here, the Ishtar Gate's glazed brick and sacred animal imagery and the Etemenanki's great height, reflect their genuine, well-documented content. Later Greek visitors to Babylon, writing after Nebuchadnezzar's time, left their own descriptions of these same structures, which historians use alongside archaeological remains to reconstruct how the city likely looked at its height.
