Hatshepsut's Punt Expedition Inscriptions
Primary Source Reading
💡 ABOUT THIS SOURCE
Adapted into plain modern English from early Egyptological translations of the relief inscriptions Hatshepsut had carved at her mortuary temple at Deir el-Bahari, recording her expedition to Punt. The reliefs combine short narrative captions with speeches placed in the mouths of the expedition's leaders and Punt's own chief.
📖 THE INSCRIPTIONS

On the purpose of the voyage:
"Sailing in the sea, setting out on the good road toward God's Land, journeying in peace toward the land of Punt, as commanded by the lord of the gods, Amun, so that the marvels of that country might be brought back for him."

The chief of Punt, Parehu, greeting the Egyptians upon their arrival:
"How is it that you have come here, to this land which the people of Egypt do not know? Have you come down from the roads of heaven, or have you sailed upon the waters of the sea of God's Land? You must have traveled the paths of the sun itself to reach us."

Describing the incense trees themselves:
"The myrrh trees of this God's Land are as fresh and flourishing as they are in their own country, brought here with their roots still in the earth, loaded onto the ships, that they might be replanted and grow in Egypt as they grow in Punt."

Cataloguing the goods loaded for the return voyage:
"The ships were loaded very heavily with the wonders of the land of Punt: every kind of good fragrant wood of God's Land, heaps of myrrh resin, fresh myrrh trees, ebony, pure ivory, gold, cinnamon wood, incense, eye-paint, baboons, monkeys, hunting dogs, and skins of the southern panther, together with the natives of the country and their children."

A closing boast, comparing the expedition to those of earlier pharaohs:
"Never was brought the like of this for any king who has ruled since the beginning of the world."
