💡 ABOUT THIS SOURCE
Excerpted and lightly adapted from public-domain scholarly renderings of the Sargon Birth Legend, a text composed and copied in Mesopotamia long after Sargon's actual reign, presented in the first person as though spoken by Sargon himself.
📖 THE TEXT

Sargon, the mighty king, king of Akkad, am I.

My mother was a high priestess; my father I never knew. My father's brothers loved the hills. My city is Azupiranu, which lies on the bank of the Euphrates.

My mother, the high priestess, conceived me, and in secret she bore me. She set me in a basket of rushes, and sealed my lid with bitumen. She cast me into the river, which did not rise over me. The river carried me along and brought me to Akki, the drawer of water. Akki, the drawer of water, took me up as he dipped his jug into the river. Akki, the drawer of water, raised me as his own son. Akki, the drawer of water, set me to his gardening.

While I was a gardener, the goddess Ishtar came to love me, and for many years I held kingship. I ruled the black-headed people, the many peoples of the land.

Mighty mountains, with axes of bronze, I destroyed. High mountain ranges I climbed over. Deep rivers I crossed. Distant lands I conquered.

Any king who comes after me, let him rule as I have ruled. Let him love the people I have loved, as Ishtar has loved me.
