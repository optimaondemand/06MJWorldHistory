💡 ABOUT THIS SOURCE
Excerpted and lightly adapted from public-domain scholarly translations of the Sumerian King List, a document compiled by Sumerian scribes from multiple ancient copies, most famously the Weld-Blundell Prism now held at Oxford. The list records the cities and kings of Mesopotamia from a legendary beginning through the more historical dynasties that followed. The extremely long reigns recorded for the earliest kings are a feature of the list itself, not a claim modern historians accept as literal fact.
📖 THE TEXT

After kingship descended from heaven, kingship was in Eridu.

In Eridu, Alulim became king and ruled for 28,800 years. Alalngar ruled for 36,000 years. Two kings ruled for 64,800 years. Then Eridu fell, and kingship was carried to Bad-tibira.

In Bad-tibira, En-men-lu-ana ruled for 43,200 years. En-men-gal-ana ruled for 28,800 years. The god Dumuzi, a shepherd, ruled for 36,000 years. Three kings ruled for 108,000 years. Then Bad-tibira fell, and kingship was carried to Larak.

In Larak, En-sipad-zid-ana ruled for 28,800 years. One king ruled for 28,800 years. Then Larak fell, and kingship was carried to Sippar.

In Sippar, En-men-dur-ana became king and ruled for 21,000 years. One king ruled for 21,000 years. Then Sippar fell, and kingship was carried to Shuruppak.

In Shuruppak, Ubara-Tutu became king and ruled for 18,600 years. One king ruled for 18,600 years.

Five cities, eight kings ruled them for 241,200 years.

Then the flood swept over the land.

After the flood had swept over the land, and kingship had descended from heaven a second time, kingship was in Kish.

In Kish, Jushur became king and ruled for 1,200 years. Kullassina-bel ruled for 960 years. Many kings followed, their reigns growing shorter with each generation, until at last a king named Etana, described as "the shepherd, who ascended to heaven and consolidated all the foreign countries," ruled for 1,500 years. The dynasty of Kish continued through more kings still, their reigns now numbered in years and tens of years rather than tens of thousands, until Kish, too, was defeated, and kingship was carried to the city of Uruk.

In Uruk, Meskiaggasher became king. After him ruled Enmerkar, who built the city of Uruk. After him ruled Lugalbanda, a shepherd. After him ruled Dumuzi, a fisherman. After him ruled Gilgamesh, whose father was said to be a spirit, and who ruled Uruk for 126 years.
